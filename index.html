10/2/19 - 6:59 am

It's much easier to run across the beach than swim across the ocean.

So if you're lost in the middle of an ocean, just look for a wave - any wave. It doesn't matter where it goes, you just need a current that'll propel you to shore. So give everything a chance. Whether they fit your narrative or not, give it a shot and see what happens because worst-case scenario is you'll end up back where you started, looking for another tide.

Once you get to shore, then you can be more peculiar with where you're situated; run over to where the sand is dryer and build the campfire there.


10/1/19 - 7:37 am

“You fall in love with the people who make you love the person you are when you’re around them.”

I don't know who to attribute this specific quote to, but phrases similar to this are thrown around here and there. It's good wisdom to keep in the back of mind as people's opinions on others typically have more to do with themselves than that other person. It's beneficial to practice also, especially if you're looking to develop social skills:

Make the other person feel good about themselves, and they will feel good about you. That's how you turn a stranger into an acquaintance, an acquaintance into a friend.


9/30/19 - 5:23 am

I was sitting in a coffee shop one day when a guy sat down directly to my left:
"Hey, how's it going?"

We ended up talking for 3 hours.

...

I remember how inspired I was, seeing how easy it was for him to greet me, a stranger. I remember it was then I realized that going up to a stranger and saying "hi" is the modern superpower - the easiest way for one to completely shift the trajectory of their life.

Imagine if you made the habit to approach strangers and just introduce yourself. Think of the potential opportunities: friendships, job opportunities, hobbies, projects, memories, etc... The easiest way to stumble upon a miracle is by someone giving it to you. The more people you know, the more opportunities for transformative intervention.

...

I've been to coffee shops many times since, but have yet to greet the stranger to my right or left.

In an earlier post, I mentioned that the first thing I do every morning is I drink a glass of water. I'm gonna change up the routine.

Before the glass of water, the first thing to do is roll the blinds, crack the window open and take in a deep breath of the fresh, morning air - in through the nostrils and out through the mouth. The 6 am air has a minty, healing power to it; it's like toothpaste for the mind. Why wait till I get to the coffee shop to breathe it in? I have the luxury to do so first thing in the morning, so that's what I'll begin to do. Besides, it's only until I step out of my room and walk back in when I realize how muggy it is at times - probably best to brush the junk out ASAP.
